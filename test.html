What is Scrum?

Scrum is a lightweight framework that helps teams work together. It encourages collaboration, accountability, and iterative progress toward a well-defined goal. Often used in software development, Scrum enables teams to deliver value incrementally and adapt to changing requirements.

At its core, Scrum is built around a set of roles, events, and artifacts that guide the team in their work. The framework emphasizes transparency, inspection, and adaptation, making it an effective approach for managing complex projects. By breaking work into manageable sprints, Scrum ensures continuous improvement and delivery.

Scrum also fosters a culture of continuous learning and adaptability. Teams regularly reflect on their processes during sprint retrospectives, identifying areas for improvement and implementing changes in the next sprint. This iterative approach helps teams stay aligned with project goals and respond effectively to evolving challenges.
